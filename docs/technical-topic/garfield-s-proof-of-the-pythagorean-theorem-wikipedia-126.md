# Garfield's proof of the Pythagorean theorem - Wikipedia

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Mathematical proof by James Garfield

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/James_Abram_Garfield%2C_photo_portrait_seated.jpg/250px-James_Abram_Garfield%2C_photo_portrait_seated.jpg)](/wiki/File:James_Abram_Garfield,_photo_portrait_seated.jpg)

Garfield in 1881

**Garfield's proof of the Pythagorean theorem** is an original proof of the [Pythagorean theorem](/wiki/Pythagorean_theorem "Pythagorean theorem") discovered by [James A. Garfield](/wiki/James_A._Garfield "James A. Garfield"), the 20th [president of the United States](/wiki/President_of_the_United_States "President of the United States"). The proof appeared in print in the *[New-England Journal of Education](/wiki/Journal_of_Education "Journal of Education")* (Vol. 3, No. 14, April 1, 1876).[[1]](#cite_note-1)[[2]](#cite_note-2) At the time of the publication of the proof, Garfield was a [congressman](/wiki/United_States_Congress "United States Congress") from [Ohio](/wiki/Ohio "Ohio"). He assumed the office of President in 1881, and served in that position until his death later that year after being [shot in an assassination](/wiki/Assassination_of_James_A._Garfield "Assassination of James A. Garfield").[[3]](#cite_note-3) Garfield is thus far the only President of the United States to have contributed anything original to mathematics. The proof is nontrivial and, according to the historian of mathematics [William Dunham](/wiki/William_Dunham_(mathematician) "William Dunham (mathematician)"), "Garfield's is really a very clever proof."[[4]](#cite_note-4) The proof appears as the 231st proof in *The Pythagorean Proposition*, a compendium of 370 different proofs of the Pythagorean theorem.[[5]](#cite_note-5)

Relationship to other proofs
----------------------------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Garfield%27s_proof_of_the_Pythagorean_theorem&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Relationship to other proofs")]

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Pythagoras_proof.svg/250px-Pythagoras_proof.svg.png)](/wiki/File:Pythagoras_proof.svg)

Garfield's proof is a variant of one of the [algebraic proofs](/wiki/Pythagorean_theorem#Algebraic_proofs "Pythagorean theorem") (pictured at right), but using only half of the diagram.
