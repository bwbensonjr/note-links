**Bitstream Charter** is a serif typeface designed by [Matthew Carter](/wiki/Matthew_Carter "Matthew Carter") in 1987 for [Bitstream Inc.](/wiki/Bitstream_Inc. "Bitstream Inc.")[[2]](#cite_note-2) Charter is based on [Pierre-Simon Fournier](/wiki/Pierre-Simon_Fournier "Pierre-Simon Fournier")’s characters, originating from the 18th century.[[3]](#cite_note-autogenerated1-3) Classified by Bitstream as a [transitional-serif](/wiki/Serif#Transitional "Serif") typeface (Bitstream Transitional 801), it also has features of a [slab-serif](/wiki/Slab-serif "Slab-serif") typeface and is often classified as such.[[4]](#cite_note-4)[[5]](#cite_note-5)

Charter was originally optimized for printing on the low-resolution 300 dpi laser printers of the 1980s, and remains suitable for printing on both modern high-resolution laser printers and inexpensive lower resolution inkjet printers due to its strong, legible design. Its structure was optimised for low-memory computers and printers. In a 2013 interview, Carter explained that it used "a very simplified structure and a minimum number of curves, more straight-line segments... very economical compared to, say, Times New Roman," but noted that rapid development of printers made this unnecessary even before he had finished the design.[[6]](#cite_note-6) In its simplification of serif forms, it foreshadowed Carter's later landmark design, [Georgia](/wiki/Georgia_(typeface) "Georgia (typeface)") for Microsoft.

In 1992 Bitstream donated a version of Charter, along with its version of Courier, to the [X Consortium](/wiki/X_Consortium "X Consortium") under terms that allowed the font to be modified and redistributed. This has resulted in open source derivatives of Bitstream Charter, including [Charis SIL](/wiki/Charis_SIL "Charis SIL").[[7]](#cite_note-7) Typographer [Matthew Butterick](/wiki/Matthew_Butterick "Matthew Butterick") considers Bitstream Charter to be one of the best free fonts available.[[8]](#cite_note-8)

Because of its popularity, a new Charter Pro release of the typeface was released in 2004, with an expanded character set including additional symbols, [ranging figures](/wiki/Ranging_figures "Ranging figures") (old-style) and small capitals.[[9]](#cite_note-9) This version was [later added as a system font](/wiki/List_of_typefaces_included_with_macOS "List of typefaces included with macOS") on [OS X](/wiki/OS_X "OS X") 10.13 High Sierra.

Carter was later asked by Monotype to consider releasing a sans-serif companion to Charter. Finding his attempts unsatisfying, he scrapped the idea for a more radical, less directly complementary design, [Carter Sans](/wiki/Carter_Sans "Carter Sans").

History
-------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Bitstream_Charter&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

Charter is based on the characters[[3]](#cite_note-autogenerated1-3) of [Pierre-Simon Fournier](/wiki/Pierre-Simon_Fournier "Pierre-Simon Fournier"), a French 18th century punch-cutter, typefounder and typographic theoretician who invented the “point system”, a standardized measurement system for font sizes.
