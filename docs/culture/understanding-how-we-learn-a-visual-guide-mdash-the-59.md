# Understanding How We Learn: A Visual Guide &mdash; The Learning Scientists

Understanding How We Learn: A Visual Guide routledge Amazon Written by Dr Yana Weinstein (Jude Weinstein-Jones) and Dr Megan Sumeracki. Book design and illustrations by Oliver Caviglioli. Published by David Fulton/Routledge, August 2018 (Copyright 2019). Educational practice does not, for the most part, rely on research findings. Instead, there’s a preference for relying on our intuitions about what’s best for learning. But relying on intuition may be a bad idea for teachers and learners alike. This accessible guide helps teachers to integrate effective, research-backed strategies for learning into their classroom practice. The book explores exactly what constitutes good evidence for effective learning and teaching strategies, how to make evidence-based judgments instead of relying on intuition, and how to apply findings from cognitive psychology directly to the classroom. Including real-life examples and case studies, FAQs, and a wealth of engaging illustrations to explain complex concepts and emphasize key points, the book is divided into four parts: Evidence-based education and the science of learning Basics of human cognitive processes Strategies for effective learning Tips for students, teachers, and parents.
