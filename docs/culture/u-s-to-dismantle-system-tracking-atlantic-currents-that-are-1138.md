# U.S. to Dismantle System Tracking Atlantic Currents That Are at Risk of Collapse - Yale E360

U.S. to Dismantle System Tracking Atlantic Currents That Are at Risk of Collapse
================================================================================

[![A mooring used in the Ocean Observatories Initiative is recovered off the coast of Alaska.](https://yale-threesixty.transforms.svdcdn.com/production/OOI_WHOI.png?w=400&auto=compress%2Cformat&fit=clip&dm=1780485909&s=e31766b1830777a59f6d8e4393702f70)](https://yale-threesixty.transforms.svdcdn.com/production/OOI_WHOI.png?w=1200&h=800&auto=compress%2Cformat&fit=crop&dm=1780485909&s=0ff86c5b80a00583326eae144da4ea50)

A mooring used in the Ocean Observatories Initiative is recovered off the coast of Alaska.
Rebecca Travis / Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution

The Trump administration is moving to dismantle an ocean observation system consisting of more than 900 instruments in the Pacific and Atlantic oceans. Data supplied by the system has been used to study key Atlantic currents that increasingly appear in danger of collapse as the climate warms.

Just days after President Trump [fired](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/apr/28/trump-fires-national-science-foundation-board) the independent board overseeing the National Science Foundation, the NSF [announced](https://oceanobservatories.org/2026/05/announcement-on-ooi-descoping/) the “removal of all in-water infrastructure” belonging to the Ocean Observatories Initiative at sites along the coasts of Oregon, Washington, Alaska, and North Carolina, and in the waters between Greenland and Iceland. Officials [say](https://oceanobservatories.org/2026/05/nsf-ooi-descoping-update-for-the-community/) the instruments will be recovered over the next 15 months.

The system, which began operating in 2016, was designed to run for at least 25 years. After just a decade in operation, the loss of monitoring instruments will leave scientists without [critical data](https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jun/02/trump-administration-ocean-observatories-initiative?utm_source=cbnewsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_term=2026-06-03&utm_campaign=Daily+Briefing+Imminent+El+Ni%C3%B1o+UK+s+87+climate+goal+States+sue+over+wind+exit) on the state of oceans and marine life. That includes data on the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation, or AMOC, a system of ocean currents that delivers warmth to northern Europe and shapes climate globally. Scientists are [increasingly concerned](https://e360.yale.edu/features/amoc-climate-change) the AMOC may be nearing a “tipping point,” after which it shuts down.

Without sustained ocean observations, “we are effectively choosing to navigate an increasingly volatile ocean with diminishing visibility,” [said Helen Findlay](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/trump-administration-takes-aim-at-crucial-ocean-monitoring-network/), of the Plymouth Marine Laboratory in the U.K. Growing uncertainty around the future of the AMOC, she said, “is precisely why long-term, consistent monitoring is more vital than ever.”

Democrats in Congress have said they will “fight” plans to dismantle the system, *The New York Times* [reports](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/02/climate/trump-ocean-monitors-climate-research.html). Senator Sheldon Whitehouse, one of the more [outspoken](https://e360.yale.edu/features/sheldon-whitehouse-interview) members of Congress on the subject of climate change, [said on X](https://x.com/SenWhitehouse/status/2061893349901025479), “Fossil fuel is heating our oceans by the zettajoule, so Trump’s corrupt fossil fuel stooges want to turn off the monitors.”
